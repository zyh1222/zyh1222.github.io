--- layout: publication year: 2025 title: "ProactiveVA: Proactive Visual Analytics with LLM-Based UI Agent" authors: - Yuheng Zhao - Xueli Shu - Liwen Fan - Lin Gao - Yu Zhang - Siming Chen highlight: true type: - Conference venue: IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Computer Graphics (VIS'25) venue_year: 2026 venue_location: venue_tags: - VIS - TVCG ccf: A ESI_highly_cited: awards: tags: - GUI agent - visual analytics - proactive agent link: "https://zyh1222.github.io/ProactiveVA/" pdf: https://zyh1222.github.io/ProactiveVA/static/pdfs/ProactiveVA_paper.pdf fast_forward: talk: demo: "https://drive.google.com/file/d/1UK2aJPcdH6E9bzVYHgy5621_NFyJa6cF/view?usp=sharing" appendix: https://zyh1222.github.io/ProactiveVA/static/pdfs/ProactiveVA_appendix.pdf slides: /slides/slides-2025-@VIS-ProactiveVA.pdf image: /images/papers/proactiveVA.png --- Visual analytics (VA) is typically applied to complex data, thus requiring complex tools. While visual analytics empowers analysts in data analysis, analysts may get lost in the complexity occasionally. This highlights the need for intelligent assistance mechanisms. However, even the latest LLM-assisted VA systems only provide help when explicitly requested by the user, making them insufficiently intelligent to offer suggestions when analysts need them the most. We propose a ProactiveVA framework in which LLM-powered UI agent monitors user interactions and delivers context-aware assistance proactively. To design effective proactive assistance, we first conducted a formative study analyzing help-seeking behaviors in user interaction logs, identifying when users need proactive help, what assistance they require, and how the agent should intervene. Based on this analysis, we distilled key design requirements in terms of intent recognition, strategy generation, interpretability and controllability. Guided by these requirements, we develop a three-stage UI agent pipeline including perception, reasoning, and acting. The agent autonomously perceives users’ needs from VA interaction logs, providing tailored suggestions and intuitive guidance through interactive exploration of the system. We implemented the framework in two representative types of VA systems, demonstrating its generalizability, and evaluated the effectiveness through an algorithm evaluation, case and expert study and a user study. We also discuss current design trade-offs of proactive VA and areas for further exploration.
