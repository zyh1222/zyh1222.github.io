Research Area

My research focuses on LLM-driven intelligent visual analytics and human-AI collaboration. I combine methods from Visualization, Artificial Intelligence, and Human-Computer Interaction to build intelligent interfaces that help people understand complex data, coordinate with AI agents, and make decisions in open-ended analytical workflows.

Selected Research Projects

2025 CellHermes: Language-based multimodal omics understanding AI for Science

Propose CellHermes, a biological large language model that integrates multimodal forms of omics data, such as transcriptomic profile and PPI network, through natural language for better omics understanding.

2025 ProactiveVA: Proactive visual analytics with LLM-based UI agents Human-AI Interaction

Designed a proactive GUI-agent framework that perceives user difficulties, reasons about assistance strategies, and acts within visual analytics interfaces to support data analysis.

2024 LightVA: LLM-agent-based task planning and visual analytics generation Visual Analytics

Built an LLM-agent workflow for decomposing analysis goals, generating visual analytics systems, and optimizing multiple linked views, reducing development from weeks to hours.

2023 LEVA: LLM-enhanced visual analytics Visual Analytics

Proposed an early LLM-enhanced visual analytics framework that supports onboarding, exploration, recommendation, and report generation. The paper is an ESI Highly Cited Paper (top 1%).

2023 ContextWing: Visual comparison for evolving social media contexts Social Computing

Developed a text comparison and visual analytics method that combines dynamic topic modeling and sequential pattern mining to reveal semantic evolution in large-scale social media streams.
